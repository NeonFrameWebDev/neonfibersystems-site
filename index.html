The whole game is one day

The day before, it’s not sellable.
The day after, it is.

Fiber doesn’t roll out all at once. A carrier turns it ON neighborhood by neighborhood, address by address. Miss the flip and you’re knocking dead doors or showing up after the competition. Catch it, and you’re the first rep on the street the day it can actually be sold.

Find the build early

We scan city construction permits and dig-locate signals daily, so we see fiber going into the ground months before the carrier’s own sales team shows up.

Watch every address

Each address near a fresh fiber permit goes on an escalating check schedule, checked more and more often as its estimated go-live approaches.

Catch the flip

The moment a door goes from not-live to live, we confirm it and hammer the whole neighborhood, then hand your reps a precise, sell-today door list.

The escalating watch

We don’t guess the go-live date.
We watch until it happens.

Go-live dates are estimates, so we treat them as estimates. Every address is re-checked on a schedule that tightens as the build matures, until the real, observed flip. No fabricated “fiber coming” flags, ever. Only checks we actually ran.

  • ✓Permit-anchored. Every lead sits within ~400 m of a real, recent fiber construction permit, not a whole-city guess.
  • ✓Honest by design. A serviceability verdict is only ever recorded from a real, observed check. Estimates are labeled as estimates.
  • ✓Flip-confirmed. The sell-today signal is the observed move from not-live to live, the strongest signal there is.
